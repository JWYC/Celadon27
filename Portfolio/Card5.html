Case Study

Willingdon Logo and Branding Guide

To capture the unique identity of a local church, I created user personas that represented the values; ideas and meanings help by the leadership and congregation. Careful consideration was given to the church's German history and reputation for excellent in studying the Bible and its international congregation. Using these factors, I decided using Palatino Linotype as the main body copy honored its German origins, and selected a clean but open and rounded word mark and font family (Quicksand) for headlines to highlights its value for modern, clarity and precision in its teaching of the Bible. With consideration for the congregation, which comes from many cultural backgrounds, the deep blue color was selected as it signals otherworldliness, peace, and religion in most other cultures. The cross as part of the logo mark is a simple line that invites the view to complete the image in their own minds, which mirrored the church's value of inviting non-believers to examine the word of God for themselves.

Expertise Used

  • Logo created in Illustrator
  • Audience Research
  • Style Guide for typography and image usage
  • Application to stationary package
  • Branded app
